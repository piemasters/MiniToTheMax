## Painting Techniques

### Armour

<Paint name="Dark Sea Blue" category="Vallejo Model Color" />
<Paint name="Russ Grey" category="Citadel Air" />
<Paint name="Fenrisian Grey" category="Citadel Air" />
<Paint name="White Scar" category="Citadel Air" />

After airbrushing the base colors I then applied an oil wash of Winsor & Newton Griffin Alkyd Raw Umber and Sansodor
over a lot of the armour, then used pure Sansodor to push the oil more into the recesses and clean the flatter plates.
I think at this point I should have applied a black oil wash into the recesses of the armour as well to increase the
contrast, but I cover that next with acrylic instead.

<Paint name="Black" category="Vallejo Model Color" />
<Paint name="Russ Grey" />
<Paint name="Fenrisian Grey" />
<Paint name="Blue Horror" />

I applied the black to the recesses, as well as a mix of black and Russ Grey to areas of the armour facing towards the
ground or that would be in shadow. I then used Russ Grey, Fenrisian Grey and Blue Horror to edge highlight all of the
armour.

<Paint name="Gory Red" category="Vallejo Game Color" />
<Paint name="Scarlet Blood" category="Vallejo Game Color" />
<Paint name="Blue Horror" />

I then used Gory Red and Scarlet Blood through the airbrush to create the glow effect from the axe. Where I lost the
edge highlights I mixed Blue Horror with Scarlet Blood and painted them back on.

<Paint name="Blue Horror" />
<Paint name="Rhinox Hide" />

To create scratches I first painted a thin straight line in one stroke using Blue Horror, applying less pressure at the
end to create a sharper point showing the direction of travel. I then painted an equally thin line above the first,
slightly overlapping it. The original line acts as the highlight to the brown scratch.

<Paint name="Blue Horror" />
<Paint name="Rhinox Hide" />
<Paint name="Doombull Brown" />
<Paint name="Mournfang Brown" />
<Paint name="Seraphim Sepia" />
<Paint name="Jokaero Orange" />
<Paint name="Fire Dragon Bright" />
<Paint name="Phalanx Yellow" />

To weather the armour, when painting a rusted divet I painted the full shape with Blue Horror, then filled it with
Rhinox Hide leaving as thin a border as possible around the edge to create the illusion of depth. When just painting
rust I instead started with Rhinox Hide. Both then follow the same steps.

Doombull Brown was used to fill two thirds of the Rhinox Hide, then Mournfang Brown to fill half the Doombull Brown.
In areas where I wanted to add a streak I then used Seraphim Sepia to create a straight line coming down from the
bottom of the chip. I then applied Jokaero Orange to the bottom of the chip and halfway down the streak.

For brighter areas of rust I added small dots of Fire Dragon Bright and Phalanx Yellow within the orange area. In some
cases I used a wash of Jokaero Orange to create a more faded look.

### Axe Blade

<Paint name="White Scar" category="Citadel Air" />
<Paint name="Fluorescent Orange" category="Vallejo Game Color" />
<Paint name="Fluorescent Magenta" category="Vallejo Game Color" />
<Paint name="Fluorescent Red" category="Vallejo Game Color" />

I airbrushed the center of the axe with White Scar, leaving the edges darker. I then applied Fluorescent Orange over the
whole blade, followed by a mix of Fluorescent Orange & Magenta focused around the blade edges. I then used
Fluorescent Red on the very edges to further darken the coolest points of the blade.

I was to redo the blade I would have followed the same steps, but would have used a yellow between the white and orange,
and would have gone to a darker red on the edges to create more contrast.

<Paint name="Scarlet Blood" category="Vallejo Game Color" />
<Paint name="Gory Red" category="Vallejo Game Color" />
<Paint name="Burnt Red" category="Vallejo Model Color" />
<Paint name="Black" category="Vallejo Model Color" />

With the airbrushing done I went on to add some further contrast. Mostly focusing on the center wolves and edge of the
blade, I used Scarlet Blood, Gory Red and Burnt Red to further darken the areas that would be cooler than the center
of the axe. I then applied small patches of Black at the very edges to create a burnt lava effect.

<Paint name="White" category="Vallejo Model Color" />
<Paint name="Fluorescent Yellow" category="Vallejo Game Color" />
<Paint name="Ice Yellow" category="Vallejo Model Color" />
<Paint name="Sun Yellow" category="Vallejo Game Color" />
<Paint name="Moon Yellow" category="Vallejo Game Color" />

Focusing on the center of the axe, in the eyes and mouths of the wolves, as well as the cracks in the blade, I thinned
down the White to get it into the recesses, then went over with the Fluorescent Yellow to create a bright glow. I left
some spots of white to create the illusion of the brightest areas, I applied Ice Yellow, Sun Yellow and Moon Yellow in
patches to a variation to the glow effect.

### Axe Glow

<Paint name="White" category="Vallejo Model Color" />
<Paint name="Fluorescent Orange" category="Vallejo Game Color" />
<Paint name="Moon Yellow" category="Vallejo Game Color" />
<Paint name="Scarlet Blood" category="Vallejo Game Color" />
<Paint name="Burnt Red" category="Vallejo Model Color" />

For areas where the axe would be casting a glow, I would pick out the sharpest highlights with white, then glaze over
them with the Fluorescent Orange, as the paint doesn't cover over darker colors well. I would then spot highlight with
yellow, then start to glaze the reds over the base material and the orange to create a gradient for the glow effect.
Sometimes a glaze back over everything with the orange would help to blend it all together.

### Skin

<Paint name="Rhinox Hide" />
<Paint name="Bugmans Glow" />
<Paint name="Pastel Blue" category="Vallejo Model Color" />
<Paint name="White" category="Vallejo Model Color" />

I applied Rhinox Hide as a base, which was then fully covered by thinned layers of Bugmans Glow. Pure Rhinox Hide is
too dark as a shadow but works well when slightly showing through behind the thin layers of Bugmans Glow. I then
mixed Pastel Blue into the Bugmans Glow to highlight the raised and larger flat areas like the forehead, nose and
cheeks. The Pastel Blue also helps to create a colder tone to the skin which I think fits the Space Wolves aesthetic.
Finally I mixed in a small amount of white and focused just below the creases in the skin to create a sharper highlight.

### Hair

<Paint name="Rhinox Hide" />
<Paint name="Dawnstone" />
<Paint name="Pastel Blue" category="Vallejo Model Color" />
<Paint name="White Scar" />

I used Dawnstone to block in the highlights over the Rhinox Hide. I then mixed the Dawnstone and Rhinox Hide to paint
the shadows, as the Rhinox is too dark for the recesses. I then mixed Dawnstone with Pastel Blue to create brighter
highlights, then added more and more White Scar into the mix to create finer highlights until using pure White Scar for
the final spot highlights.

### Leather

<Paint name="Rhinox Hide" />
<Paint name="Jokaero Orange" />
<Paint name="Morghast Bone" />
<Paint name="Mournfang Brown" />

I added thin scratchy lines of Jokaero Orange over the Rhinox Hide, focusing on the edges, to create a worn leather
effect. I then used Morghast Bone to add some sharper highlights. Finally I used Mournfang Brown as a thin glaze to
blend the scratches a bit more into the base color and add a bit of warmth to the leather.

### Bone

<Paint name="Rhinox Hide" />
<Paint name="XV88" />
<Paint name="Morghast Bone" />
<Paint name="Screaming Skull" />
<Paint name="White" category="Vallejo Model Color" />

The colors are painted on using very small scratchy brush strokes to create a texture that resembles bone, much shorter
than the lines used to create the leather effect. The Agrax Earthshade was applied very thinly as a glaze to help tie
all the colors together.
